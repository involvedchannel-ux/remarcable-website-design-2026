25 Metric Tons of Waste: That's How Much This Art Installation Aims to Bring Awareness To

Remarcable · 9th June, 2023 · 3 min read
Article Image

Dental retainers have revolutionised dental care — but as the popularity of clear aligners grows across global markets, so does the concern for the environmental impact they create. Could greater awareness prevent it from spiralling further out of hand?

We've all seen how dentistry has changed over the past two decades. From expensive treatments to at-home solutions, modern machinery and technology have opened up access to better dental care for patients across the globe. But as the industry advances, so does the question of what it leaves behind.

The Problem

Dental retainers have revolutionised dental care by providing a discreet and efficient alternative to traditional braces — however, as the popularity of clear aligners grows, so does the environmental concern they generate.

Each year, an estimated 25 metric tons of waste is generated from discarded dental retainers.

Recognising the need to address this, the innovative team at Banning Dental Clinic partnered with us to embark on a project that not only enhances their patients' experiences but contributes to sustainability efforts in an industry that is often more concerned with profits than initiatives.

A Real Head Turner

When the award-winning team at Banning Dental & Skin Clinique sought to highlight their retainer area in their new Blackfriars location, they realised that existing graphic elements weren't captivating patients' attention or encouraging interaction.

Inspired to create a space that aligned with their Instagram-able interior, they set out to explore ideas that would resonate with their brand and incorporate sustainability.

Being a dental practice that generates a strong proportion of its sales through retainer work, Banning was well positioned to lead on this — and to stand as proof that even one practice, at a time, can make a meaningful difference.

Enter the Skull Artwork

Our experts envisioned an art installation that would showcase the journey of clear dental retainer treatment at Banning in a way that would stop even the least engaged visitor in their tracks. Extensive research revealed that patients choose clear aligners for their discreteness, removability, shorter treatment period, and natural appearance — but often struggle to envision how they might look on them personally. Building on this insight, we recognised the value of allowing patients to see, touch, and experience the aligners firsthand.

Instead of using standard ceramic teeth to display the aligners, we proposed something far more striking — a skull.

The skull's association with rebellion, uniqueness, and individuality perfectly resonated with the clinic's mission to create a stand-out, non-generic element of surprise.

Installation Image
The skull artwork installation at Banning Dental & Skin Clinique, Blackfriars, London.

A Fusion of Art and Sustainability

With the skull as their canvas, the team created visually striking artwork symbolising different stages of an Invisalign treatment journey. But they didn't stop at aesthetics.

Recognising the environmental urgency, sustainability was woven into every aspect of the project — from the craftsmanship of the skulls themselves to the recycling apparatus beneath each one, designed to accept used retainers during treatment.

Moving Forward

Now fully launched and collecting recycled retainers, the installation stands as a proof of concept: that design can drive environmental behaviour change. It also raises a broader question for an industry that has benefited enormously from advances in materials and technology — what responsibility do we carry for what those advances leave behind?

We understand the world needs to start weighing solutions not only by their effect on the end-user, but on the environment we all share.
