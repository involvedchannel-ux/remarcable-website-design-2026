What Does the Next Generation of Healthcare Design Look Like?

Remarcable · 17th November, 2020 · 2 min read
Article Image

2020 has prompted us to reconsider much of what we took for granted. Heightened health safety measures are driving lasting change — and nowhere is this more significant than in the design of clinical and healthcare spaces.

While the conversation rightly focuses on clinical technology and operational protocols, the quality of the physical environment is often overlooked. The pandemic has begun to shift that. The key to a successful design lies in the needs of its users — both staff and clients alike. Technical spaces must function efficiently, but this shouldn't come at the cost of physical and mental wellbeing. We first need to think about the emotional needs of patients and staff as a baseline, and then build upon that with personalised branding.

Sense of Calm

Patient anxiety before appointments is universal. A well-designed environment can shift that emotional state before a word is spoken. Stepping into a thoughtfully designed space creates an automatic response — a sense of safety, of care, of being in good hands. This isn't incidental. It's the whole point.

Customer Experience

Service quality drives patient loyalty and repeat visits. The design details — from the texture of the seating to the coherence of the wayfinding — create a positive experience while differentiating your practice from competitors. Consistency reinforces trust. Trust creates habit. Habit creates the foundation of a sustainable practice.

Safety

Health safety is now a permanent feature of the sector, not a temporary response. Ultra-adaptable spaces incorporating considered social distancing, sensor technology, air filtration, and UV sterilisation demonstrate a visible commitment to patient wellbeing. This is both a practical necessity and, increasingly, a marketing advantage.

Combining these three factors — aesthetically, coherently, and in a way that reflects your organisation's values — is the real challenge of healthcare interior design. It is also where great design justifies every penny of its investment.
